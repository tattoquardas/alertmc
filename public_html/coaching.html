COACHING

The coaching and tele-coaching services provided by Alert Management Consultants are structured in consultation with the client and carefully tailored to his or her needs. While face-to-face coaching sessions (which usually last 60 to 90 minutes; to a maximum of 2 hours) have proven most effective, it may be necessary to conduct some sessions via telephone or webcam to accommodate restrictions posed by the client’s work-related or private circumstances. Sessions will be held as far as possible outside the client’s regular work environment.


Focus of the coaching services

The coaching programme focuses on the development and implementation of strategies that help the client to achieve goals that the individual has set for him- or herself in relation to professional performance or personal fulfilment. The coaching may centre on specific personal projects, work-life balance, job performance or job satisfaction, or on more general circumstances in the client’s personal life, business or field. A variety of methods and techniques may be used during coaching, including personal strategic planning, values clarification, brainstorming, motivational counselling and other instruments. The main objective is to develop new strategies that allow the participant to successfully cope with specific challenges, as determined at the beginning of the coaching programme in consultation with the various parties involved (stakeholders).


Throughout the coaching process, the different stakeholders are expected to contribute to a constructive coaching experience and outcome, to work towards building trust and to respect all agreements made with regard to accountability and confidentiality. As a client, you determine which aspects will be focused on. The programme’s success depends in part on your willingness to take risks and try new approaches. The coach-client relationship is relatively candid and challenging. Your coach strives to be honest and straightforward, to ask incisive questions and to keep challenging you to move forward. Over the course of the coaching programme, you will be asked to evaluate which progress has been made so far. If you are unsatisfied with any aspect of the coaching process, please notify me as soon as possible so that we can take joint steps to correct the problem.


Number and frequency of sessions:

Ten 1-hour face-to-face coaching sessions. The programme starts with a meeting, conference call or video conference with the different stakeholders to jointly determine the client’s coaching goals.


Optional (recommended): Assessment and Development Centre

This comprehensive assessment programme starts with a half-day, face-to-face session, during which we make an inventory of the client’s present situation (strengths/weaknesses, style, personal values and motives, etc.), the requirements of his or her current position and the complex organisational dynamics in which he or she operates. The Assessment Centre programme includes exercises like an in-basket test with observers, as well as an analysis of the Koan-PI Personality Test.


Confidentiality

As coaches, we are ethically and legally obliged to safeguard the confidentiality of our communications. Information collected in the context of a coaching programme may only be shared with authorised third parties if it clearly relates to the client’s organisation and the client’s previously-established coaching goals.


Points of departure:

Anything discussed between client and coach during a coaching session is treated as strictly confidential. The progress reports will be structured according to the specifications set out in the section that deals with the coaching structure.

Clients remain personally responsible for any progress and successes achieved within the coaching programme. The coach is neither willing nor able to guarantee that a client will take specific actions or achieve specific targets.
