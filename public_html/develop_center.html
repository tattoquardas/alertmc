DEVELOPMENT CENTRES

A Development Centres (DC) serves a similar purpose to an Assessment Centre, in that it is used to determine the individual’s specific strengths and weaknesses. However, in a Development Centre, the focus is on which competences the candidate could build on to accelerate his or her professional development.

The programme ultimately yields a Personal Development Plan (PDP) that includes recommendations for the individual’s future growth. The objective is to consistently stimulate the candidate to improve his or her skills and strive towards further growth and success in his or her career. The Development Centre also results in a custom Plan of Action, which is drawn up by the candidate him- or herself – with support from Alert Management Consultants.

Alert Management Consultants helps organisations to set up and implement a Development Centre for their staff – as well as the associated follow-up actions. This support is offered in the context of:

- The progression of management team members to a higher level
- The identification of high-potential candidates who can be appointed to complex positions of responsibility, or in the context of a management development programme.
- Strength/weakness analyses of organisations’ members of staff: mapping out the domains in which the candidate is relatively strong, as well as the areas in which he or she needs to be supported, redirected or coached.
- Career development programmes that are geared towards helping employees to reposition their career within the organisation.

2. Development Centre

A Development Centre may consist of the following elements:
- 360°Feedback: This element is based on a survey held within the candidate’s immediate professional environment (colleagues, managers, team members). The survey yields quantifiable data regarding the candidate’s performance in the context of his or her existing position.
- Personality Test (KOAN-PI): Provides insight into how likely it is that the candidate will demonstrate specific competences in future roles.
- Facet Intelligence Test: Provides insight in the candidate’s capacity to solve numerical and verbal.
- In-basket test with roleplaying: Provides insight in the candidate’s handling of complex situations, how he or she takes decisions and sets priorities, and whether the candidate has the potential to effectively handle roles specific to the position.
- Interview: Provides insight in the extent to which the candidate has already demonstrated particular competences in the past.
- Group exercise: This element can be included in the Development Centre programme if different employees from the same organisation will be taking part in a DC on the same day.
- ALTI / MBTI: If the commissioning company already uses this self-report questionnaire within its own organisation, it may be useful to include it in the DC programme. Another instrument frequently used in the context of career- and performance-related questions is ALTI. Knowing the candidate’s psychological type can help create added value. In view of the descriptive nature of these instruments, ALTI and MBTI should not be used within programmes that are strictly geared towards selection or promotion.
- Feedback (mandatory): At the end of the session, we provide the candidate with extensive personalised feedback regarding his or her performance.


3. Communication of results

The results of all Development Centre elements will be communicated to you by means of an extensive report. We will consult with you beforehand to jointly determine the precise reporting format. Naturally, this report will be treated with strict confidentiality, and we will fully respect the ownership of the various details contained within.
